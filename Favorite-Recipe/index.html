Baklava

A crispy, flaky pastry filled with nuts and topped with a honey syrup!

Ingredients

  • For the Baklava
    • 8oz filo pastry
    • 6oz walnuts
    • 6oz pistachios
    • 2 tbsp sugar
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 1 tsp ground cloves
    • 4oz butter
  • For the Syrup
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1/4 cup honey
    • 1 tbsp lemon juice
    • 1/2 orange zest
    • 3 cloves

Directions

  1. If the pastry is frozen, defrost it overnight in the fridge, in the original packaging.
  2. First prepare the filling by finely chopping the walnuts and pistachios, then mixing both together with the sugar, cinnamon and cloves. Divide the nut filling into 3 small dishes for ease later.
  3. Have a pastry brush and baking dish ready, around 26x18cm. Cut one piece of filo pastry into quarters.
  4. If you prefer, you can get all the pastry ready by cutting 10 sheets into quarters, but be aware you need to keep them covered until needed or they will become dry and brittle. Alternatively, keep the pastry in the original wrapping and cut a sheet as needed. If you need to trim the pastry, then you can use trimmings to make a layer now and then.
  5. Melt the butter. Be aware you may need to melt it again as you go, since it is likely to harden while you are building the baklava.
  6. Lightly brush the baking dish with butter then place one piece of the filo pastry in the bottom in a single layer. Working quickly, and keeping any pastry you are not using either in the original wrapping or under a damp cloth, brush the pastry in the dish lightly with butter then add another layer on top. Repeat until you have 10 layers of pastry in the dish.
  7. Next, add one of the dishes with the nut filling (ie â…“ of the total) and spread over the pastry.
  8. Add another layer of pastry, brush with butter, then repeat adding additional layers. The first layer after the nut filling is always a little tricky to brush so make sure it is one that is not brittle and hold it slightly on one edge as you brush. Once again, add 10 layers.
  9. Around this point, preheat the oven to 350F/175C.
  10. After the 2nd 10 layers of pastry, add another layer of nuts, then another 10 layers of pastry, layer of nuts, and a final layer of pastry. In total, you will have 4 sets of 10 sheets of pastry, with 3 layers of nut filling in between.
  11. Once you have finished building the baklava, brush the top with butter. If you have a gap at the edge of your dish, fill this with crunched up foil. Score the baklava before cooking, cutting at least the top layer of pastry.
  12. Bake uncovered for approximately 45 minutes, or up to 60 minutes, until it is golden on top.
  13. While the baklava is baking, prepare the syrup. Place the water, sugar, honey, orange zest and cloves in a small pan and warm gently over a medium-low heat. Stir so that everything dissolves and mixes. Once it comes to a boil, simmer for a couple minutes then set aside. Remove the orange zest and cloves and stir in the lemon juice before using.
  14. Once the baklava is golden on top and the layers of pastry appear cooked, remove from oven and pour over the syrup while it is still hot. Do this carefully as it may sputter, and try to spread the syrup evenly over the baklava. Let it cool and enjoy.
